Design principles

Five non-negotiable laws. If a screen breaks one, the screen is wrong — not the law.

01

Clarity over chrome

Motion, elevation and ornament must make a state change legible — or be removed. Nothing decorates; everything explains.

02

Audit-as-feature

The per-record Activity timeline is a designed product surface, not a raw audit log. Who did what, when, and what changed — readable at a glance.

03

Scope always visible

The active tenant and subsidiary are shown in the shell at all times. An agent never has to guess which book of business they are acting on.

04

Deny-wins, felt gracefully

Every block or denial is a calm state that offers the next legal action. A wall, never a dead end — and never a scolding.

05

Optimistic but honest

Actions apply instantly. When they fail, they roll back with the most visible motion in the system and a plain toast. Speed is never paid for in trust.

03 · Semantic tones

StatusPill gallery

Five tones, each mapped to a design-system tone token. A pill’s tone comes only from this map — and always carries a text label.

ToneMeaningFillText · border · dot
neutralNo state / default--iso-n-100--iso-fg-muted · --iso-n-300 · --iso-n-600
infoIn progress, informational--iso-info-soft--iso-brand · --iso-blue-3-300 · --iso-accent
successDone, healthy, won--iso-success-soft--iso-green-800 · --iso-green-300 · --iso-success
warningNeeds attention, at risk--iso-warning-soft--iso-yellow-800 · --iso-yellow-300 · --iso-warning
dangerBlocked, failed, lost--iso-danger-soft--iso-red-700 · --iso-red-300 · --iso-danger

Status is never carried by colour alone. Every pill pairs its tone with a text label and a tone-matched dot, so meaning survives colour-blindness, greyscale and low contrast. This is an accessibility floor, not a style choice.
